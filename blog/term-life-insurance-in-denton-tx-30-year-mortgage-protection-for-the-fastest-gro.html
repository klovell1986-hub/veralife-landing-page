FAQ — Denton Term Life Insurance (6 Q&As)

Q1: How much term life insurance does a family with a $480,000 mortgage need?

A: A standard recommendation is 10–12x annual income plus outstanding mortgage debt. For a Denton family with a $480k mortgage and $100k household income, a $1M–$1.5M 30-year term policy would cover mortgage, income replacement, and education costs.

Q2: Is a medical exam required for term life in Texas?

A: Many carriers offer no-exam term life up to $500k–$1M for applicants under 50. Above that or for the best rates, a paramedical exam (blood draw and urine sample at your home) is typically required.

Q3: Can I lock in a rate for 30 years?

A: Yes. Level-premium term life guarantees your monthly payment stays the same for the full term — 10, 15, 20, or 30 years. After the term ends, you can convert to permanent coverage or let the policy expire.

Q4: Does Texas have any special rules for life insurance beneficiaries?

A: Yes. Texas is a community property state, which means your spouse generally has a legal interest in life insurance policies purchased with community funds. If you name someone other than your spouse as beneficiary, your spouse's written consent may be required. This is important for Denton families to understand when setting up beneficiary designations.

Q5: What happens if I move from Denton to another state during my term?

A: Your premium stays the same, and coverage continues without interruption. Term life is portable across state lines. If you move frequently for work (common in the DFW corporate transfer market), your policy moves with you at the same rate.

Q6: Should both parents buy term life, or just the primary earner?

A: Both parents should have coverage. If the stay-at-home or lower-earning parent dies, replacing their labor — childcare, transportation, household management — costs $40,000–$80,000 per year in the DFW metro. A $250,000–$500,000 term policy on the non-earning parent is a small investment for critical protection.
