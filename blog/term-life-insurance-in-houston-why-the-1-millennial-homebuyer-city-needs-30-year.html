FAQ — Houston Term Life Insurance

Q: Do I need a medical exam for term life insurance in Houston? A: For face amounts above $100,000, most carriers require a paramedical exam (blood, urine, blood pressure). No-exam term policies are available up to $250,000 with 20–30% higher premiums. For Houston residents in good health, accepting the exam saves $15–$30/month on a $500,000 policy. Q: Can I get term life insurance in Houston if I have a pre-existing condition? A: Yes, depending on severity. Conditions like well-controlled asthma, mild anxiety, and controlled high blood pressure typically qualify for standard rates. More serious conditions (cancer history, Type 1 diabetes) may result in a rate surcharge (25–150% above standard). An independent agent can match your profile to the carrier most favorable to your condition. Q: What happens to my term life policy if I move out of Texas? A: Your policy is fully portable — it stays in force regardless of where you live. Your premium and death benefit are locked in at issue. Notify the carrier of your address change, but no policy modification is needed. Texas residency does not affect your policy terms. Q: How is the death benefit from term life insurance taxed? A: Life insurance death benefits are received income tax-free by beneficiaries under IRC Section 101(a). This applies regardless of the policy size or relationship to the deceased. Texas has no state income tax, so there is no state-level tax on life insurance proceeds.

3. Houston's Weather Risk and the Need for AD&D Riders

Houston's hurricane and flood risk creates a specific underwriting consideration: standard term life covers all causes of death including natural disasters, but an Accidental Death & Dismemberment (AD&D) rider can be worth adding for Houston residents.

Texas weather deaths (2024–2025 average):
  • Hurricane-related fatalities: 15–30/year statewide
  • Flash flood deaths: 10–20/year in Harris County
  • Heat-related deaths: 100–150/year across Texas
  • Traffic fatalities: Houston leads Texas metros with 300+/year
AD&D rider cost: $2–$5/month per $100,000 of additional accidental death coverage. For a Houston homeowner, a $250,000 AD&D rider adds roughly $5–$12/month and doubles the payout if death occurs due to accident.

4. Houston's Health and Underwriting Landscape

Texas health demographics and term life underwriting:

  • Obesity prevalence: 35% of Texas adults — standard rates for BMI up to 33
  • Tobacco use: 13% of Texas adults smoke — non-tobacco rates save 55–65%
  • Hypertension: 32% of Texas adults — controlled BP is a standard qualifier
  • Diabetes: 11.2% of Texas adults — well-managed type 2 qualifies for standard+ with select carriers
  • Air quality: Houston's ozone levels don't affect life insurance underwriting
  • Cancer rates: Harris County has a slightly elevated rate of certain cancers (petrochemical corridor) — cancer survivors in remission for 5+ years typically qualify for standard rates
Simplified issue in Texas: Policies up to $1M are available with simplified underwriting — no blood draw, no medical exam. Approval in 24–72 hours. For the busy Houston professional who doesn't want to schedule a paramedical visit, this is the path of least resistance.

5. Hurricane Preparedness and Life Insurance

Houston homeowners should know: your life insurance policy isn't affected by hurricane or flood risk. Life insurance premiums are based on age, health, and tobacco use — not your home's flood zone. You can live in a 100-year floodplain in Meyerland and pay the same term life rate as someone in The Woodlands.

What IS affected by Houston weather:
  • Homeowners insurance: Flood and windstorm are excluded or require separate policies
  • Auto insurance: Comprehensive coverage handles flood damage to vehicles
  • Life insurance: Not affected — no premium difference based on location within Texas

This means there's no reason to delay buying term life in Houston due to weather concerns. Your rate is locked by your age and health, not your ZIP code.

5. Riders Worth Considering for Raleigh Families

Child Term Rider — $10–$20/year per $10k

This rider covers final expenses for children and is extremely inexpensive. For Raleigh families with young children, it provides peace of mind for roughly $1–$2/month per $10,000 of coverage.

Accelerated Death Benefit (Critical Illness) — often free

If diagnosed with a qualifying critical illness (cancer, heart attack, stroke), you can access up to 50% of your death benefit while living. This is typically included at no additional cost in modern term policies.

Waiver of Premium — $2–$5/month

If you become totally disabled and can't work, this rider keeps your policy in force without premium payments. For Raleigh's high-income dual-earner households, losing one income is catastrophic enough — this ensures the life insurance doesn't lapse on top of it.

6. North Carolina's Regulatory Environment

NC has specific rules that affect term life buyers:

  • Free look period: 10 days to review and cancel for a full refund
  • Incontestability period: 2 years — after that, carrier cannot deny a claim except for fraud
  • Grace period: 31 days to pay overdue premium before lapse
  • Replacement rules: If replacing an existing policy, the agent must provide a comparison document

These protections make NC one of the stronger consumer-protection states for life insurance buyers.
