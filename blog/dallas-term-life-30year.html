1. What Is Term Life Insurance and Why Dallas Families Need It

Term life insurance is straightforward: you lock in a fixed premium for a set period — most commonly 10, 20, or 30 years — and if you pass away during that term, your beneficiaries receive a tax-free death benefit. No investment component, no complexity — just pure protection during the years your family needs it most.

The case for term life in Dallas is especially strong:

  • Population boom: DFW added 170,000+ new residents in 2025, with the majority being working-age adults and families under 40
  • Home prices: The median home price in the Dallas metro sits at ~$370,000, with 30-year fixed mortgages as the standard
  • Dual-income households: Over 60% of Dallas married-couple families are dual earners — losing one income can be catastrophic
  • Group life gap: Most Dallas employers offer group term life at 1–2x salary, but that's rarely enough. A $60,000 benefit doesn't cover a $370,000 mortgage

What affects your rate?

  • Smoking: Tobacco users pay 2–3x more. Quitting for 12+ months qualifies for non-tobacco rates.
  • Health class: Preferred Plus (best rates), Preferred, Standard Plus, Standard. A clean health record with no major conditions puts you in the preferred tiers.
  • Family history: Certain cancers or heart disease before age 60 in immediate family may affect classification.
  • Occupation and hobbies: High-risk work or avocations (aviation, racing, SCUBA) can increase premiums.

Accelerated Death Benefit Rider: Most carriers in Texas include this rider at no additional cost. If you're diagnosed with a terminal illness (typically 12 months or less to live), you can access up to 50–100% of your death benefit early. This is standard on most Banner/Legal & General, Protective, and Pacific Life policies available through VeraLife.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take my term life policy if I move out of Dallas or Texas?

Yes. Term life insurance is portable nationwide. If you relocate for a job transfer to Austin, Charlotte, or anywhere else, your policy stays in force as long as you continue paying premiums. VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) serves clients across the U.S. — your coverage doesn't change when you change zip codes.

Can I convert my term life policy to permanent coverage?

Most term policies offered through VeraLife include a conversion privilege. This allows you to switch to a whole life or universal life policy — typically without a medical exam — during a specified window (usually the first 5–15 years of the term). Ask your VeraLife agent about conversion terms for each carrier.

What if I don't want a medical exam?

Several carriers offer accelerated underwriting for applicants under 50 with clean health histories. Protective Life and Banner/Legal & General are the strongest in Texas for no-exam approvals. If you qualify, you can be approved based on prescription database and MIB checks alone. VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) can pre-screen your eligibility.

Should I get a 20-year or 30-year term?

For most Dallas families under 40, the answer is 30-year term. A 30-year fixed mortgage, children who will be in college in 20+ years, and a peak earning window that extends into your 50s all favor the longer term. The premium difference is smaller than most people expect — sometimes as little as $10–$15 more per month to double your coverage window.

Can I have more than one term life policy?

Absolutely. Layering multiple policies is a common strategy. For example: a 30-year $500,000 policy to cover the mortgage and a 20-year $300,000 policy for income replacement during peak child-rearing years. This lets you reduce coverage as obligations decrease, without overpaying for a longer single policy.

Isn't my employer coverage enough?

Most employer group life policies in Texas provide 1–2x your annual salary — typically $50,000–$150,000. That covers final expenses but leaves a significant gap for mortgage payoff, income replacement, and education. Plus, if you leave your job, the coverage ends. Individual term life closes that gap and is owned by you, not your employer.
