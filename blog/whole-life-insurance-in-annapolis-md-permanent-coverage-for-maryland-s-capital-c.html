FAQ — Annapolis Whole Life Insurance

Q: Do I need a medical exam for whole life?

A: Most whole life policies require a medical exam for face values over $100,000. Simplified issue options are available for lower amounts.

Q: Can I buy whole life for my child?

A: Yes. Many carriers offer whole life policies for children. This locks in insurability and builds cash value over decades.

Q: Will my policy pay dividends?

A: Participating whole life policies from mutual insurance companies pay non-guaranteed dividends. These can be taken as cash, used to reduce premiums, or buy additional paid-up insurance.

Q: What happens if I stop paying premiums?

A: The policy's cash value can be used to pay premiums (automatic premium loan provision). If the cash value is depleted, the policy lapses.
