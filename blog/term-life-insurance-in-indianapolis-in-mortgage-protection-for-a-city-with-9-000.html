Indianapolis-Specific Considerations

Indianapolis is in the middle of a construction boom. The city issued over 9,000 single-family building permits in 2025 — a 10-year high — fueled by population growth, relocations from higher-cost Midwest cities (Chicago, Detroit), and a thriving logistics and life sciences economy anchored by Eli Lilly, Salesforce, and the FedEx Mid-Atlantic hub.

But here's the challenge: Indiana has the 5th-highest mortgage debt-to-income ratio in the Midwest. The typical Indianapolis homeowner carries a mortgage payment that consumes 28–32% of household income. If the primary earner dies, that mortgage becomes an immediate threat to the surviving family's housing stability.

Term life insurance is the most cost-effective solution. A level-premium 30-year term policy matches a standard 30-year mortgage, guaranteeing the loan is paid off if the primary earner dies during the term. For a 35-year-old Indianapolis homeowner with a $250,000 mortgage, $250,000–$500,000 in term coverage costs $35–$65/month and provides:

  • Mortgage payoff — The full death benefit pays off the remaining mortgage balance
  • Income replacement — For a family earning $75,000/year, 10x income = $750,000 in term coverage, leaving 3–5 years of living expenses after the mortgage is paid
  • College funding — For families with children, an additional $100,000–$200,000 per child covers in-state tuition at IU, Purdue, or Ball State

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much term life coverage do I need for my Indianapolis mortgage?

A good starting point is your remaining mortgage balance. For Marion County's median home value of $230,000 with a typical $200,000 remaining balance, a $250,000 policy covers the mortgage entirely. For full family protection including income replacement and education, use 10–12x your annual income plus the mortgage balance.

Q: Can I get term life insurance in Indianapolis without a medical exam?

Yes. Most carriers offer simplified issue term life up to $1,000,000 with no medical exam — just a health questionnaire and in some cases a brief phone interview. Policies above $1,000,000 typically require a paramedical exam (blood draw and urinalysis), which can be done at your home.

Q: Does Indiana have a state income tax that affects life insurance?

Indiana imposes a flat 3.15% state income tax. However, life insurance death benefits are exempt from both federal and state income tax under IRC Section 101(a). Policy loans and cash value growth are not taxable income. Indiana does not have a state estate or inheritance tax.

Q: What happens if I outlive my term policy?

When a term policy expires, coverage ends with no payout. You can typically convert to a permanent policy (whole life or universal life) at your attained age without a new medical exam, though premiums will be higher. Many Indianapolis homeowners in their 40s and 50s ladder multiple term policies at different lengths to match declining mortgage balances.
