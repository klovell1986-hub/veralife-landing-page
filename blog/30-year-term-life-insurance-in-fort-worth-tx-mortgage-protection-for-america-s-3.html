5. Texas-Specific Considerations

  • Texas has no state income tax — life insurance death benefits are also federally tax-free
  • Texas homestead law protects primary residences from creditors — but the mortgage is still due
  • No medical exam requirement — term policies under $250,000 may qualify for simplified issue
  • Tornado/hail risk — does not affect life insurance premiums in any way
  • Texas Department of Insurance regulates all carriers — verify licensure before purchasing

FAQ — Fort Worth Term Life Insurance

Q: Should I get term life before or after buying my home?

A: Before or simultaneously. You want coverage in place when the mortgage closes. Many homebuyers apply for term life during the mortgage application process.

Q: Can I buy a policy that decreases with my mortgage balance?

A: Yes — that's called decreasing term life or mortgage protection insurance. However, level term (where the benefit stays constant) is generally recommended because it provides more flexibility and the premium difference is small.

Q: What if my mortgage is paid off before the 30-year term ends?

A: Your term life policy continues for the full 30 years at the same premium. You can keep the coverage or cancel it (with no refund of unused premium). Many families keep it for income replacement even after the mortgage is paid.

Q: Can I name my spouse as beneficiary even if we're divorcing?

A: You can change beneficiaries at any time. If your situation changes, update your beneficiary designation with the insurance company.
