1. What Is Term Life Insurance and Why Raleigh Tech Families Need It

Term life insurance provides a guaranteed death benefit for a specific period at fixed, level premiums. It's the most efficient way to protect your family's financial future during your highest-earning years.

Why Research Triangle families are especially exposed:

  • High single-income dependence — many tech households rely on one primary earner
  • Employer coverage caps at 1-2x salary — group policies are not designed for true income replacement
  • High housing costs — median home price in Raleigh ~$435,000, mortgage is the biggest liability
  • Job hopping is common — group coverage doesn't follow you when you leave; individual policies do
  • Dual-income tech households — when both spouses earn six figures, the loss of either income can be devastating

A $1,000,000 20-year term policy for a healthy 30-year-old in Raleigh costs approximately $85-$130/month. That's a 30-minute meeting with an agent that protects $1 million of your family's future. Compared to the $1.44 million gap that a typical Apple or Google engineer has, this is one of the most cost-effective financial decisions you can make.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I have life insurance through my employer. Do I still need an individual term life policy?

A: Yes, for most tech professionals. Employer group life typically covers only 1-2x your salary — leaving a massive gap for income replacement and mortgage protection. Additionally, employer coverage ends when you leave your job. An individual term policy is portable and stays with you regardless of your employer.

Q: Can I get term life insurance without a medical exam?

A: Yes. Simplified issue policies are available up to $1,000,000 with just a few health questions. For larger amounts ($1M+), a medical exam typically results in better rates. A VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) agent can help you decide which path is most cost-effective.

Q: What happens to my term life policy if I move to another state?

A: Your term life insurance is fully portable. It stays with you wherever you live in the United States. Premiums and coverage remain unchanged. This is especially important for tech professionals who may relocate as their career progresses.

Q: Can I have more than one term life policy?

A: Absolutely. Many Raleigh tech professionals use layered coverage — a 30-year $1M policy for mortgage protection and a 20-year $1M policy for peak income replacement during their highest earning years. This can be more cost-effective than a single large 30-year policy.

Q: What riders are worth adding to my term life policy?

A: The most valuable riders for tech professionals are waiver of premium (coverage continues if you become disabled) and accelerated death benefit (access funds if terminally ill). Child term riders and accidental death are also popular, typically adding $5-$15/month each.

Q: How does VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) help me choose the right policy?

A: We compare rates and underwriting guidelines across multiple A-rated carriers to find the best fit for your age, health profile, and coverage needs. Since each carrier specializes in different risk profiles, shopping across carriers typically saves 15-30% compared to applying directly.

Q: Do I need to designate a trust as my beneficiary for estate planning purposes?

A: If your estate approaches the federal exemption limit ($13.99M in 2026), naming a trust as beneficiary can help with estate tax planning. For most Raleigh tech professionals with coverage of $1M-$3M, naming individual beneficiaries (spouse, children) is sufficient.

Q: Does term life insurance cover suicide?

A: Most policies include a suicide exclusion clause for the first two years. After the two-year contestability period, the full death benefit applies. This is a standard provision across virtually all carriers.
