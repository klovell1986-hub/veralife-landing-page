1. What Is Term Life Insurance and Why Houston Families Need It

Term life insurance pays a tax-free death benefit if you die during a set period (the "term"), typically 10, 20, or 30 years. It's the most affordable type of life insurance because it has no investment component — you're paying purely for protection.

Why Houston families specifically need it:

  • #1 city for millennial homebuyers — more young families with mortgages than any other US city
  • Energy, healthcare, and aerospace economy — high-paying jobs mean high incomes to replace
  • Texas suburbs are booming — Katy, Sugar Land, Pearland, The Woodlands all growing rapidly
  • Standard 30-year mortgages — the most common loan product for first-time buyers
  • Harris County alone added over 45,000 new residents in 2025 — the pipeline of young families needing protection keeps growing

A $500,000 30-year term policy for a healthy 30-year-old Houston resident costs approximately $50-$80/month. That's less than a dinner out twice a month for $500,000 of mortgage and income protection. When you consider that the policy replaces your income for a decade or more if something happens, the value proposition is extraordinary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What happens to my term life policy if I move out of Houston or Texas?

A: Term life insurance is fully portable. Your coverage stays with you regardless of where you live in the United States. Your premiums remain level and your death benefit remains guaranteed, even if you relocate to another state.

Q: Can I convert my term life policy to permanent insurance later?

A: Most carriers offer conversion privileges that allow you to convert your term policy to a permanent whole life or universal life policy without a new medical exam. This is valuable if your health declines during the term period and you want to extend coverage beyond the initial term.

Q: Do I need a medical exam for term life insurance?

A: Not necessarily. Simplified issue term policies are available up to $1,000,000 with no medical exam — just a few health questions. However, fully underwritten policies (with a medical exam) typically offer lower rates for healthy applicants. VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) can help you decide which approach saves you the most money.

Q: What is the difference between 20-year and 30-year term life?

A: A 20-year term is approximately 15-25% cheaper per month but expires sooner. For Houston homebuyers with a standard 30-year mortgage, a 30-year term aligns perfectly — the coverage lasts as long as the mortgage. If you're within 20 years of retirement, a 20-year term may be sufficient.

Q: Can I have multiple term life policies?

A: Yes. Many Houston families layer policies — a 30-year term to cover the mortgage and a 20-year term to cover income during peak earning years. This can be more cost-effective than a single large 30-year policy.

Q: What riders should I consider for my term life policy?

A: Popular riders include waiver of premium (policy stays in force if you become disabled), accelerated death benefit (access funds if terminally ill), child term rider (small policy on each child), and accidental death benefit. Most of these add $5-$15/month to your premium.

Q: Is term life insurance through my employer enough?

A: Group life insurance through your employer typically covers only 1-2x your salary. For a Houston tech or energy professional earning $120,000, that covers $120,000-$240,000 — far less than the $1,000,000+ needed to replace a decade of income and pay off a mortgage. Employer coverage also ends when you leave the job. An individual term life policy is essential for adequate protection.
