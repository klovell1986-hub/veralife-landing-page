Maricopa County accounts for 40% of Arizona's total population, with Phoenix at its center as the fastest-growing major metro in the US. Arizona gains approximately 200 new residents daily — many affluent retirees.

Arizona's community property laws present a special consideration. Assets acquired during marriage are owned equally. Whole life insurance owned by one spouse can provide tax-free liquidity outside the probate estate.

For Phoenix-area retired couples with appreciated primary residences (median: $450,000), investment accounts ($500K–$1.5M), and Social Security, the combined estate frequently exceeds $2M.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How does Arizona's community property law affect whole life insurance?

A: In Arizona, any asset acquired during marriage is presumed community property. A policy can be structured with one spouse as sole owner for clean beneficiary distribution.

Q: Do Phoenix retirees need whole life with pension income?

A: Yes. Pensions provide income but don't create estate assets for wealth transfer. Whole life creates an immediate, tax-free inheritance.

Q: What coverage amount should Phoenix couples consider?

A: A common strategy is $100k–$250k per spouse for final expenses and equalization; $250k–$500k for estates above $2M.
