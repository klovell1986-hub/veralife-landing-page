Market Overview

Frisco, Texas, is growing at a pace that defies comparison. With a 4.2% population growth rate in 2025 (the highest of any US city by percentage), Frisco is adding families faster than it can build schools, roads — and insurance coverage.

The typical Frisco household earns a median income of $135,000+ (exceeding $150k in some ZIPs like 75034 and 75035). These are dual-income professional families — tech managers, healthcare administrators, DFW airport executives, and entrepreneurs. They carry high expectations for lifestyle, education, and financial security.

The Mortgage Gap Problem

Frisco's real estate market is unique in Texas. Homes in The Star, Phillips Creek Ranch, and Panther Creek routinely sell for $650k–$950k. The surviving spouse must not only cover the mortgage principal but also maintain the tax payments on a single income.

A 30-year term life policy — covering the full mortgage term — is the standard recommendation for any Frisco homeowner with children.

3 FAQs

Q1: Is a 30-year term life policy better than what my employer offers? For most Frisco professionals, yes. Employer-provided group life:

  • Disappears when you leave or lose your job
  • Has no portability guarantee
  • Cannot be converted to permanent coverage in most cases

A personal 30-year term policy covers you for the full mortgage period, is owned by you (not your employer), and goes with you if you switch jobs. It also costs less per thousand dollars of coverage than group buy-up rates.

Q2: I'm 28 and just bought my first Frisco home. Should I get term life now? Absolutely. The best time to buy term life is when you are young, healthy, and insurable. A 30-year term for a 28-year-old Frisco homebuyer with a $500k mortgage costs roughly $22–$28/month. If you wait until 40, that same policy costs 80–100% more. Locking in the rate now saves tens of thousands of dollars over the life of the policy.

If your job transfers you to Austin, Charlotte, or Denver, the policy stays with you at the same premium.
