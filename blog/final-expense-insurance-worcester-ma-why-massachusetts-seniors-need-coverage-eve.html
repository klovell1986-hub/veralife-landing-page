FAQ — Worcester Final Expense Insurance (5 Q&As)

Q1: Will my final expense policy be subject to the Massachusetts estate tax?

A: No. Massachusetts has a $1.66M estate tax threshold, and final expense policies typically range from $5,000–$25,000 — well below that threshold. The death benefit passes income-tax-free and estate-tax-free to your beneficiary.

Q2: Can I buy a policy if I already have health issues?

A: Yes. Final expense insurance uses simplified underwriting — no medical exam required, just 3–6 health questions. Most conditions like controlled diabetes, high blood pressure, or past surgeries won't disqualify you.

Q3: Are final expense benefits taxed as income in Massachusetts?

A: No. Life insurance death benefits are not subject to federal or Massachusetts state income tax. The full benefit is paid directly to your beneficiary, typically within 24–48 hours of claim approval.

Q4: Can I buy final expense insurance if I'm over 80?

A: Yes. Many carriers offer final expense coverage up to age 85, and some go to age 90. Worcester seniors can still apply and qualify at older ages. Premiums are higher at older ages, but a $10,000 policy at age 80 typically runs $117–$145/month — still manageable on most fixed incomes.

Q5: What happens to my policy if I move to another state?

A: Your policy remains in full force. Final expense insurance is portable across state lines. If you relocate from Worcester to Florida or another state, the policy continues at the same premium. Just notify the carrier of your new address. This is a major advantage over Massachusetts prepaid funeral contracts, which are tied to a specific funeral home.
