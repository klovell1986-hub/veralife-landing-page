Market Overview

Alexandria, Virginia sits at the epicenter of the federal contracting economy. With $150 billion+ in concentrated federal contractor wealth and the highest density of security-cleared executives outside the District of Columbia, Alexandria's Old Town, Potomac Yard, and Carlyle neighborhoods are home to some of the most asset-rich households in the Mid-Atlantic. Here, 38% of households have $750,000 or more in investable assets — and many face a specific financial challenge that term insurance can't solve.

The challenge: estate tax exposure and concentrated wealth in qualified plans. Alexandria's executives — think retired senior military officers now serving as defense contractors, former intelligence community leaders, and cleared program managers — often hold significant deferred compensation, TSP accounts, and federal pensions. When they die, these assets pass to a surviving spouse with no estate tax (thanks to the marital deduction), but when the surviving spouse dies, the combined estate can trigger federal or state estate taxes. Virginia has no state estate tax (repealed in 2007), but the federal exemption ($14.07 million in 2026) can be exceeded when you add together a home in Old Town ($1.5M+), investment accounts, deferred comp, and a life insurance payout.

The solution: whole life insurance held in an Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust (ILIT). An ILIT removes the death benefit from the insured's taxable estate, ensuring the proceeds go to heirs free of both income and estate tax. For Alexandria executives with federal pensions that stop at death (meaning no survivor benefit for adult children), an ILIT-owned whole life policy creates a tax-free inheritance that replaces lost pension value.

Additionally, whole life's guaranteed cash value growth provides a conservative asset in a portfolio that may otherwise be over-concentrated in government contracting stock or real estate. For security-cleared executives whose income is tied to contract cycles and federal budgets, whole life's premium certainty and non-market-correlated growth offers stability that variable investments cannot.

How to Buy Whole Life with an ILIT in Alexandria, VA

  1. Work with an estate planning attorney first. An ILIT is a legal structure that must be properly drafted. Your life insurance agent should coordinate with your attorney, not replace them. The ILIT must be established before the policy is applied for — owning the policy from inception avoids the three-year lookback rule.
  2. Size the policy to cover estate tax exposure. Calculate current net worth + projected growth + existing life insurance. If the combined total is near or over the federal exemption, structure the ILIT-owned whole life to cover the estimated estate tax bill — and fund it with sufficient annual premium through the Crummey power provisions.
  3. Choose the right carrier for your health profile. Alexandria executives in their 40s and 50s may have borderline health markers. An independent agent shops multiple carriers to find the one that will approve you at the best health rating — potentially saving thousands per year.
  4. Structure the pay period. For executives in peak earning years (ages 45–60), a 10- or 15-pay whole life policy allows you to complete premium funding before retirement, when your cash flow transitions from W-2 income to pension and TSP distributions.

3 FAQs

1. Why do Alexandria executives need an ILIT for whole life insurance? Without an ILIT, the life insurance death benefit is included in your taxable estate. For an Alexandria executive with a $2M home, $2M in retirement accounts, and a $2M life insurance policy, the estate is $6M — well under the federal exemption. But add deferred comp ($1M+), a consulting practice ($1M+), and investment accounts ($1M+), and suddenly the estate is $9M–$12M. With growth, the next spouse's combined estate could hit or exceed the exemption. An ILIT locks in the proceeds as estate-tax-free — now, not later. It's cheap insurance against future tax law changes, and it ensures 100% of the death benefit reaches your heirs.

2. Can I fund an ILIT whole life policy with my C‑corp or S‑corp? Indirectly. The corporation cannot own the policy if the ILIT is involved (that creates transfer-for-value issues and corporate-owned life insurance complications). However, the corporation can bonus compensation to the executive, who then makes cash gifts to the ILIT via Crummey powers to pay premiums. This is a common structure for Alexandria's government contractor owners and should be coordinated with your CPA and estate attorney.

3. Are there better options than whole life for Alexandria executives with significant deferred comp? It depends on your goal. If your primary concern is maximizing investment returns, a variable universal life (VUL) policy offers market-linked growth with tax-deferred accumulation. However, VUL carries market risk and the cash value is not guaranteed. If your primary concern is guaranteed estate liquidity and tax-free wealth transfer with no downside risk — the scenario most relevant to federal contractor executives with significant deferred comp and pension assets — whole life in an ILIT is the gold standard. It's the only strategy that guarantees a specific death benefit regardless of market conditions.
