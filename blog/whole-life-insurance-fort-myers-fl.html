Market Overview

Lee County, anchored by Fort Myers and Cape Coral, has become Florida's premier destination for affluent retirees relocating from the Northeast and Midwest. An estimated 30% of all new residents to Lee County arrive with a net worth exceeding $1 million, drawn by Florida's warm climate, absence of a state income tax, and comparatively lower property tax burden relative to the Northeast.

This demographic shift is reshaping the local insurance market. Incoming retirees carry significant assets — appreciated primary residences sold in New York, New Jersey, Illinois, or Massachusetts; substantial IRA and 401(k) balances; and in many cases, business equity from sold companies. When combined with existing Florida real estate holdings, many of these households sit in the $2 million to $10 million net worth bracket — precisely the range where traditional estate planning solutions fall short.

Florida imposes no state estate tax — a major advantage over the 12 states (plus D.C.) that do. However, the federal estate tax exemption is scheduled to sunset at the end of 2025, dropping from roughly $13.6 million per individual to approximately $7 million per individual (adjusted for inflation). For Lee County couples with combined estates of $8–15 million, that means potential federal estate tax exposure of hundreds of thousands to over a million dollars.

Whole life insurance — particularly premium-financed whole life — is the most efficient way to create immediate estate liquidity without liquidating concentrated asset positions. A $2 million policy can be structured with annual premium outlays far below the face amount, or in some cases, financed entirely through a third-party lender, securing the estate's liquidity need for less than the cost of a single year's tax liability.

  • Pacific Life — Industry leader in premium-financed life insurance. Strong institutional relationships with banks that serve as premium finance lenders. Competitive on $1M–$10M face amounts.

How to Buy Whole Life Insurance in Fort Myers, FL

  1. Evaluate your estate tax exposure. Estimate current and projected net worth under the reduced federal exemption. For most Lee County couples worth $5–15M, the planning window is now.
  2. Determine the liquidity gap. Illiquid assets (real estate, business interests) cannot easily fund a tax payment due in 9 months. The shortfall = the face amount you need.
  3. Choose between self-pay and financing. Self-pay works up to $2M. Premium financing suits $2M+ coverage: a bank funds premiums using the policy's cash value as collateral. At death, the carrier repays the loan and the balance goes to heirs.
  4. Select the policy structure. Options: participating whole life (mutual dividends), indexed whole life (market-linked growth), or survivorship whole life (covers both spouses, pays at second death).
  5. Complete full underwriting. Blood work, EKG (60+ or cardiac history), and a financial review may be required — especially for financed cases.

3 FAQs

Q1: I'm a retiree moving from New York to Fort Myers. Should I buy whole life before or after the move? Buy after establishing Florida residency. Florida's insurance regulatory environment is favorable, and there is no Florida estate tax — but more importantly, buying as a Florida resident avoids the complexities of a multi-state underwriting file. To establish residency, obtain a Florida driver's license, register to vote in Lee County, and maintain a Fort Myers address for at least 90 days before applying. If you own a home in both states, discuss with your agent whether to structure the policy on a split-dollar or trust-owned basis.

Q2: Can whole life insurance be owned by an irrevocable life insurance trust (ILIT) in Florida? Yes — and for Fort Myers residents with estate tax exposure, an ILIT is often the correct ownership structure. An ILIT removes the death benefit from your taxable estate, ensuring the proceeds are available for estate liquidity without increasing the estate tax burden. Florida law specifically permits ILITs, and several Fort Myers trusts and estates attorneys specialize in their setup. The ILIT must be established and funded (via premium gifts or premium financing) at least three years before death to avoid the three-year lookback rule.

Q3: How does premium financing work for a $3 million whole life policy in Fort Myers? A bank lends the annual premium to the ILIT, using the policy's cash value as collateral. The borrower pays only the loan interest — ~$15,000–$25,000/year on a $3M policy, versus $75,000+ for the full premium. At death, the carrier pays the benefit, the loan is repaid, and the remainder passes to beneficiaries. The key risk: if cash value underperforms loan interest accruals, additional collateral may be required.

Conclusion

Fort Myers has become ground zero for affluent retiree migration on Florida's Gulf Coast. With 30% of new residents carrying $1M+ net worth and the federal estate tax exemption set to cut nearly in half, Lee County families in the $2M–$10M bracket face a significant liquidity planning gap.

Whole life insurance — deployed through premium financing for larger cases — creates immediate, guaranteed liquidity that no other financial instrument can match. It is the most capital-efficient way to fund an estate tax bill without disrupting a carefully built portfolio of Florida real estate, retirement accounts, and business interests.

The estate planning window is open now. Rates, health qualifications, and premium financing terms favor applicants who act before the exemption sunset and before age-related increases make coverage more expensive.
