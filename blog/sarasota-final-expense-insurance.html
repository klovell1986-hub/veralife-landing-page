How to Buy Final Expense in Sarasota

Step 1: Determine the coverage amount. Calculate the total expected cost: funeral ($5,000–$8,000), burial or cremation ($2,000–$5,000), cemetery plot or columbarium niche ($3,000–$8,000), headstone or marker ($1,000–$3,000), and incidental costs (obituary, transportation, memorial service) at $500–$2,000. The target coverage amount for most Sarasota seniors is $10,000–$15,000.

Step 2: Choose simplified issue over guaranteed issue if possible. Simplified issue asks 3–5 health questions and typically has no waiting period for full coverage. Sarasota seniors with managed chronic conditions (hypertension, type 2 diabetes, well-controlled heart disease) will likely qualify for simplified issue at standard rates. Guaranteed issue is best for those with serious diagnoses (cancer, dementia, recent stroke) but includes a 2-year graded benefit period.

Step 3: Compare rates across at least 3 carriers. Final expense pricing varies significantly between carriers for the same age and coverage amount. An independent agent who represents multiple carriers can provide a side-by-side comparison.

Step 4: Verify the carrier's A.M. Best rating. Florida's guaranty association provides protection up to $300,000 in life insurance benefits, but choosing a carrier with an A- (Excellent) or higher rating from A.M. Best provides an additional layer of financial security.

Step 5: Designate beneficiaries. Florida law requires specific beneficiary designations. Most Sarasota seniors name adult children or a trust as beneficiaries. Policies that name a trust require coordination with the trust document and should be reviewed by an estate planning attorney.

FAQs

Q: Are final expense benefits taxable in Florida?

A: No. Florida has no state income tax, and life insurance death benefits are exempt from federal income tax. Beneficiaries receive the full policy amount tax-free regardless of the amount.

Q: Can Sarasota seniors get coverage if they take multiple medications?

A: Yes. Simplified issue policies typically ask about specific conditions (cancer, heart disease, stroke, diabetes, COPD) rather than medication count. Many seniors who take medications for managed conditions qualify at standard rates. Guaranteed issue is available for those who cannot answer the health questions affirmatively.

Q: What is the graded benefit period on guaranteed issue policies?

A: The standard graded benefit period is 2 years. If the insured dies of natural causes within the first 2 years, the beneficiary receives only the premiums paid plus a small interest payment (typically 5–10%). After 2 years, the full death benefit is payable. Accidental death is covered from day one.
