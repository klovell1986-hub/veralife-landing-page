3. Whole Life vs. Term for Warren Families

When term life wins:
  • Young families with 30-year mortgages
  • Parents with children under 18
  • Temporary needs (mortgage protection, income replacement)
  • Lower budget for insurance
When whole life wins:
  • Permanent coverage needs (final expenses, estate planning)
  • Retirees or near-retirees
  • Maxed out retirement contributions and need tax-advantaged growth
  • Business owners with key person or buy-sell needs
  • Legacy planning — guaranteed death benefit for heirs

FAQ — Warren Whole Life Insurance

Q: Can I convert my existing term policy to whole life?

A: Most term policies have a conversion rider. You can convert to whole life without a new medical exam up to a certain age (typically 65–70).

Q: Is the cash value guaranteed?

A: Yes. Whole life policies have guaranteed cash value schedules. Participating policies may also earn dividends (not guaranteed) that increase cash value.

Q: What happens to my whole life policy if I move to another state?

A: Whole life is fully portable. You keep the policy regardless of where you live, as long as premiums are paid.

Q: Do I need a medical exam for whole life?

A: Policies over $100,000 typically require a paramedical exam. Simplified issue policies ($25,000–$100,000) are available with health questions only.
