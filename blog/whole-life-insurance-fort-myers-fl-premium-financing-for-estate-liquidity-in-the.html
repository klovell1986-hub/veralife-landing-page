FAQ — Fort Myers Whole Life Insurance (6 Q&As)

Q1: I'm a retiree moving from New York to Fort Myers. Should I buy whole life before or after the move?

A: Generally, buy before you move if your current health is good. Once you move, go through a Florida-licensed agent for the policy. Rates are based on your health at application, not your state of residence.

Q2: How much whole life coverage do Fort Myers retirees typically need?

A: Most affluent retirees target $500k–$2M in whole life coverage — enough to cover estate taxes, provide legacy for heirs, and access cash value during retirement if needed.

Q3: Can I access the cash value of my whole life policy while living in Fort Myers?

A: Yes. Whole life policies accumulate tax-deferred cash value you can borrow against or withdraw. Many retirees use this for supplemental retirement income, emergency funds, or long-term care needs.

Q4: Does Florida tax whole life policy benefits?

A: No. Florida has no state income tax, so life insurance death benefits are tax-free at the state level. The death benefit is also income-tax-free at the federal level when paid to a named beneficiary. This makes whole life one of the most tax-efficient wealth transfer tools available to Florida residents.

Q5: What if I want coverage but am over 70?

A: Whole life is available to age 85 with most carriers, but premiums at older ages are significantly higher. At age 70, a $500,000 policy runs $17,500–$22,000/year. An alternative for older buyers is to consider a smaller whole life policy combined with a term policy, or a universal life policy with simpler underwriting.

Q6: Can I name a trust as my beneficiary in Florida?

A: Yes. Many Fort Myers retirees with estates exceeding $1M name an ILIT (Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust) as the beneficiary. This keeps the death benefit out of your taxable estate — particularly important since Florida has no state estate tax but federal estate taxes still apply above $13.61M (2026). Consult with a Florida estate planning attorney before setting up an ILIT.
