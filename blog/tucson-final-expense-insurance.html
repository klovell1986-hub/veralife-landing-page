Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I qualify for final expense insurance with pre-existing conditions like diabetes or heart disease?

A: Yes. Simplified issue final expense policies ask only 3-5 health questions. Many carriers offer favorable rates for applicants with well-controlled conditions. Guaranteed issue policies have no health questions at all, making coverage accessible to virtually anyone aged 50-85.

Q: How is the death benefit paid out to my beneficiary?

A: Once a certified death certificate and completed claim form are submitted, most carriers issue a lump-sum payment within 24 to 48 hours. The benefit is income tax-free and can be used for any purpose — funeral costs, outstanding bills, or everyday expenses.

Q: Is final expense insurance available to Tucson residents under age 50?

A: Most final expense products start at age 50. For younger Tucson residents, term life insurance is typically more affordable and provides higher coverage amounts. An agent at VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) can help you determine which product fits your needs.

Q: Does the policy build cash value?

A: Yes. Final expense insurance is a whole life product, which means it accumulates cash value over time on a tax-deferred basis. You can borrow against this cash value or surrender the policy for its accumulated value if circumstances change.

Q: Can I add a spouse or family member to my policy?

A: Some carriers offer spousal riders that allow you to cover both spouses under a single policy, though separate policies are more common and often more cost-effective. Each spouse should have their own independent coverage to ensure benefits are available regardless of who passes first.

Q: What happens if my health changes after I buy a policy?

A: Your premiums are locked in at the time of purchase and never increase, even if your health declines. The death benefit is guaranteed as long as premiums are paid. This is one of the key advantages of buying final expense insurance early — you lock in rates based on your current health.
