Market Overview

Colorado Springs isn't just the second-fastest-growing city in Colorado — it's one of the fastest-growing metros in the entire Rocky Mountain region. The city grew 2.5% in 2025 alone, and nearly 50% of those new arrivals are military-affiliated families connected to Peterson Space Force Base, Schriever Space Force Base, or Fort Carson. That means thousands of young families with SGLI (Servicemembers' Group Life Insurance) coverage in the $400,000–$500,000 range — and a $300,000–$500,000 income-replacement gap once you factor in the cost of raising a family in a metro where starter homes now begin at $425,000.

Here's the reality: SGLI is a phenomenal benefit while you're on active duty. It's cheap, it's guaranteed, and it pays fast. But it's also temporary. Once you separate, transition to the Reserve, or retire, SGLI converts to VGLI (Veterans' Group Life Insurance) — and the premiums climb steeply with age. Worse, SGLI coverage maxes out at $500,000, which is barely enough to cover a mortgage, college costs, and living expenses for a young family in today's Colorado Springs market.

That's where a private 20- or 30-year term life policy comes in. It fills the gap SGLI leaves open. It locks in healthy rates while you're young and fit. And it stays with you after the uniform comes off — no conversion, no medical exam at re-entry, no premium shocks.

For Colorado Springs families, the math is straightforward: $425,000 starter home × a 30-year mortgage + college for two kids at UCCS ($60,000+ each in 2026 dollars) + 10 years of living expenses = $1.2 million in coverage need at minimum. SGLI covers less than half of that. Private term life closes the gap.

Carrier Landscape

Colorado Springs residents benefit from strong competition among carriers with military-friendly underwriting. Corebridge Financial (formerly AIG) offers one of the most competitive 30-year term products in the region, with favorable rates for veterans with service-connected disabilities rated at 0% or mild. Banner Life / Legal & General consistently lands near the top of the rate charts for large face-amount policies ($750k+). Pacific Life has excellent conversion provisions — important for service members who want the option to convert to permanent coverage after separation. Mutual of Omaha offers accelerated underwriting for applicants under age 50 with clean health histories, meaning Colorado Springs families can often get approved in under two weeks without a paramedical exam.

For military families specifically: carriers vary on how they treat deployments, overseas residencies, and combat-related health issues. A knowledgeable independent agent can help you navigate which carrier's underwriting guidelines are most favorable given your specific service history. Not all carriers view a deployment to CENTCOM the same way.

How to Buy Term Life in Colorado Springs

1. Assess your SGLI/transition timeline. If you're still active duty, your SGLI is the baseline. Buy a private policy now — while you're still young and healthy — that will replace SGLI upon separation. This avoids the VGLI premium spike.

2. Work with an independent agent. VeraLife (NPN: 21426840) works with multiple carriers, not just one. That means we compare underwriting guidelines alongside premiums to find the policy that will actually approve you at the best rate.

3. Choose the right term length. Match your term to your dependency period. 30-year term is ideal if you have children under 5 — it covers them until they're financially independent. 20-year term works if your mortgage is the primary concern and your kids are older.

4. Lock in now. Term life rates increase with age. A 30-year-old male in Colorado Springs pays roughly $63/month for $1M of 30-year term. At 40, that same policy jumps to $112/month. At 50, $258/month. Every year you wait costs you.

6 FAQs

Q1: Does SGLI count as life insurance for my family's needs, or do I need a separate policy?

SGLI is a solid base, but it's insufficient for most Colorado Springs military families. The $500,000 SGLI max might cover your mortgage and a year of expenses, but it won't replace 10-15 years of your income plus college tuition. A private term policy fills the gap — and because you're younger and healthier now, you'll lock in a lower rate than waiting until separation.

Q2: Can I get term life insurance while deployed or with a combat-related injury?

Yes — and you should, while on active duty when you're typically in excellent health. Some carriers may place temporary moratoriums on deployment zones, but an independent agent can find the carrier whose deployment policy works with your timeline. For post-deployment injuries, many carriers offer standard or preferred rates if the condition is well-controlled and doesn't affect long-term mortality. VeraLife has experience placing coverage for active-duty and recently separated service members in Colorado Springs.

Q3: What happens to my term life policy if I move out of Colorado Springs?

Nothing. Term life insurance is a nationally portable contract. If you receive PCS orders to another base or decide to move after separation, your policy moves with you at the same premium. That's a key advantage over employer-provided coverage and SGLI, both of which end when your affiliation ends.

Q4: How do term life rates in Colorado compare nationally?

Colorado's insurance market is competitive, and El Paso County residents generally pay rates close to national averages. The state's higher altitude and active outdoor lifestyle mean approved health classes tend to be favorable — Colorado ranks among the top states for percentage of residents at preferred-plus health class. This can translate to lower rates for fit, non-smoking applicants.

Q5: What term length should a military family with young children choose?

For families with children under 10, a 30-year term is strongly recommended. It covers through college graduation even if you're starting your family later. For example, an E-7 at age 32 with two children ages 3 and 1 would want 30-year coverage to protect them through ages 33 and 31 — well into their independent years.

Q6: Can I convert my term life policy to permanent coverage later?

Many carriers offer conversion privileges — you can convert your term policy to a whole life or universal life policy during the term without a new medical exam. This is valuable if you develop a health condition later and still want permanent coverage. Check your policy for conversion deadlines; most require conversion before age 65 or within the first 10 years.

Conclusion

Colorado Springs is growing fast, and so are its military families. SGLI was never designed to be a permanent, standalone solution — it's a group benefit for active-duty service members. To protect your spouse, your children, and the home you're building, a private term life policy is the smartest financial move you can make. The younger and healthier you lock it in, the better.
