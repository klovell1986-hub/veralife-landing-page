Tampa-Specific Considerations

Tampa's housing boom has transformed Pasco and Hillsborough Counties over the past five years. The average 35-year-old Tampa homeowner now carries a $370,000 mortgage — but only $55,000 in employer-provided group life insurance. That $315,000 gap would devastate most families if the primary breadwinner dies unexpectedly.

Florida's status as a homestead exemption state provides some creditor protection for primary residences, but it does nothing to cover ongoing living expenses, childcare, or college costs. Term life insurance proceeds provide tax-free liquidity that surviving spouses can use to pay off the mortgage or fund the household without selling the family home.

With 2.1% annual population growth and a median home price of $415,000 in Hillsborough County, Tampa families need term life coverage that matches their mortgage timeline. A 30-year term purchased at age 35 covers through retirement age — exactly when the mortgage will be paid off and children will be independent.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I get term life insurance in Florida with a pre-existing condition like diabetes?

A: Yes. Well-managed conditions often qualify for standard or preferred rates. For $400k/30yr term, Banner Life and Corebridge both offer competitive rates for applicants with controlled Type 2 diabetes or mild hypertension.

Q: Should I buy term life through my employer or privately in Tampa?

A: Employer group life (typically 1x salary) is a good supplement but not a primary solution — it's tied to your job and coverage limits are low. Private 30-year term life is fully portable and stays active even if you change employers.

Q: Is the death benefit taxable for Florida residents?

A: No. Life insurance death benefits are received income-tax-free under IRC Section 101(a). Florida has no state income tax, so beneficiaries receive the full death benefit amount with no federal or state tax liability.
