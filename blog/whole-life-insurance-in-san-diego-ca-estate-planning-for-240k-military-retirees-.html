San Diego-Specific Considerations

San Diego County is home to 240,000+ military retirees — the largest concentration of any metropolitan area in the US outside Washington, DC. Combined with San Diego's thriving biotechnology and defense sectors, this creates a unique demographic mix.

California does not impose a state-level estate tax, but the federal $13.99M exemption (2025–2026) applies. For San Diego County families with appreciated real estate — median home values exceed $890,000 — plus retirement accounts and investment portfolios, the combined estate can approach the exemption threshold faster than most realize.

San Diego's high cost of living — roughly 41% above the national average — makes term life attractive for income replacement, but whole life serves a distinct role in estate equalization and wealth transfer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How does whole life insurance work for estate planning in California?

A: Whole life provides a guaranteed death benefit that passes to beneficiaries income-tax-free, and accumulates cash value on a tax-deferred basis.

Q: Is whole life better than a trust for estate planning?

A: They serve different purposes. A trust controls asset distribution and avoids probate but does not create new funds. Whole life creates immediate, tax-free liquidity at death.

Q: Can military retirees qualify for whole life with VA benefits?

A: Yes. VA benefits and whole life are complementary — SGLI coverage ends after military separation, and whole life fills the gap for family protection.
