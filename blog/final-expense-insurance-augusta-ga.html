Market Overview

Augusta, Georgia is a city known for the Masters Tournament, the Savannah River, and its growing retiree population. Richmond County — the heart of Augusta — has 23% of residents aged 65 or older. And that number is accelerating: the senior population in the Augusta metro area is projected to grow 18% by 2028, driven by medical retirees drawn to Augusta's world-class healthcare (home to Augusta University Medical Center, Piedmont Augusta, and the VA's Charlie Norwood Medical Center) plus military retirees from Fort Eisenhower (formerly Fort Gordon).

The financial reality for Augusta seniors: the average funeral cost in Georgia is $7,100 — and rising 4–6% annually. A traditional funeral with viewing, burial plot, casket, and service can easily reach $10,000–$12,000 in the Augusta market. Cremation, which accounts for 55% of Georgia's end-of-life arrangements, lowers costs but still averages $3,500–$5,000 with memorial service and urn.

Despite these clear costs, only about 35% of Augusta seniors have final expense insurance. That means two out of three seniors in Richmond County have no dedicated funds for their final arrangements — leaving adult children to scramble, sell assets, or go into debt. Georgia has no inheritance tax (it fully repealed its estate tax in 2012), so there's no tax penalty on passing assets to heirs — but there's also no state-level incentive to pre-plan. The burden falls on families.

Final expense insurance — also called burial insurance — solves this precisely. It's a small whole life policy (typically $5,000–$25,000) designed to cover funeral costs, outstanding medical bills, and minor debts. It is:

  • Guaranteed acceptance options available (no medical exam, just health questions)
  • Level premiums that never increase with age
  • Immediate death benefit (some policies have a 2-year graded benefit for certain health conditions)
  • No forced liquidation of savings or investments at death

For Augusta seniors on fixed incomes (Social Security + pension), final expense premiums of $30–$80/month are manageable. The benefit? Their children don't have to come up with $8,000+ at a moment's notice.

Carrier Landscape

The final expense market in Georgia is highly competitive, and Augusta seniors benefit from strong carrier options. Mutual of Omaha is the market leader in Georgia for final expense, offering level benefits from day one (no graded period for preferred health). Their application is straightforward, and they are known for fast claims payment — critical for funeral homes awaiting payment. For Augusta's significant military retiree population, certain carriers offer discounts or more favorable underwriting for veterans. Given the high concentration of Fort Eisenhower retirees (many with service-connected disability ratings), working with an agent who understands VA benefits and how they interact with insurance underwriting is critical.

How to Buy Final Expense in Augusta, GA

1. Get your funeral cost estimate first. Call three local funeral homes (e.g., Thomas Poteet & Son, Platt's Funeral Home, Elliott Sons) and ask for a general price list. Federal law requires them to provide it. Use this to set your coverage amount — don't guess.

2. Choose simplified issue over guaranteed issue if your health allows. Simplified issue (health questions) gives you immediate full coverage. Guaranteed issue (no health questions) has a 2-year graded benefit — meaning if you pass away in year one, only your premiums plus 10% are returned. If you can answer "no" to the typical 6–8 health questions, simplified issue is the better value.

3. Name a beneficiary who can handle the funds quickly. While the policy pays to your estate, naming an individual (adult child, spouse) as beneficiary means the money is available in 2–5 business days — not tied up in probate for months.

3 FAQs

1. Do I need final expense insurance if I have other life insurance?

It depends. Your existing term or whole life policy is likely too large to leave instructions for funeral use — and it may be designated for mortgage, income replacement, or college costs. Final expense is a small, dedicated policy that says "this money is for my funeral." It keeps the big policy doing its job while ensuring your children aren't paying $7,000–$12,000 out of pocket. Many seniors use both: term/whole life for their spouse, final expense for immediate end-of-life costs.

2. Does Georgia Medicaid have a funeral asset limit?

Georgia's Medicaid program allows a pre-paid funeral contract (an irrevocable burial trust or funeral insurance policy assigned to a funeral home) that is exempt from the asset limit — up to a reasonable amount. This means you can pre-fund your funeral through an insurance policy that Medicaid won't count toward the $2,000 asset limit. However, the policy must be irrevocable and assigned to a funeral home. Always verify current rules with your elder law attorney or Georgia's Division of Aging Services.

3. What happens if I move from Augusta to another city or state — does my final expense policy still work?

Yes. Final expense life insurance policies are nationally portable. Your beneficiary receives the death benefit regardless of where you live or where you pass away. However, if you pre-funded a specific funeral home through an assignment, that arrangement is specific to that funeral home. The insurance itself stays with you anywhere in the U.S.
