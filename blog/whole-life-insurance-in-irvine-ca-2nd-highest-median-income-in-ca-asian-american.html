FAQ — Irvine Whole Life Insurance

Q: What coverage amount do Irvine professionals typically need?

A: For dual-income Irvine households with $1.1M+ home values and equity compensation, $500k–$1M per spouse is common. This covers estate tax exposure, provides legacy liquidity, and builds meaningful cash value. A good rule of thumb: coverage equal to 2–3x annual household income plus mortgage balance.

Q: How does whole life cash value compare to a 401(k) or IRA?

A: They serve different purposes. A 401(k) is for retirement accumulation with tax-deferred growth; whole life provides guaranteed cash value growth, tax-free death benefits, and policy loan access without credit checks. In Irvine's high-income environment, both are complementary — max your 401(k) employer match first, then add whole life for the permanent layer.

Q: Do Irvine families really need whole life?

A: Yes. For dual-income tech families with appreciated real estate and equity compensation, whole life provides estate liquidity term life cannot.

Q: What about RSU compensation?

A: RSUs create concentrated wealth requiring estate planning. Whole life can hedge against stock value decline at death.

Q: What coverage is recommended?

A: $250k–$500k per spouse is typical. Dividend-paying whole life has historically provided 5–6% total returns on cash value.

Q: Can I convert existing term life to whole life?

A: Many term life policies include a conversion rider allowing you to convert to whole life without a new medical exam. This is popular among Irvine professionals who initially bought term for mortgage protection but now want permanent coverage. Check your policy's conversion deadline — most require conversion before age 70 or within the first 10 years.
