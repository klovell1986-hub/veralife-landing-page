Headline

[Final Expense Insurance in Tampa: 20% of Hillsborough County Residents Are Veterans — Here’s What the VA Doesn’t Pay]

TL;DR

  • Hillsborough County has ~99,000 veterans (20% of the adult population), yet only 40% are enrolled in VA health care benefits
  • The VA burial allowance covers up to $1,591 for non-service-connected deaths — far below Tampa’s $9,000–$12,000 average funeral cost
  • A $10,000 final expense (burial insurance) policy costs $48–$90/month depending on age and fills the gap that VA benefits cannot
  • Graded, level, and guaranteed issue plans are available for Tampa seniors ages 50–85
  • Florida National Cemetery (Bushnell) provides free plots for eligible veterans, but families still pay for funeral services, caskets, urns, and transportation

Tampa-Specific Considerations

Tampa’s veteran population is one of the highest concentrations in Florida. MacDill Air Force Base — headquarters for U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) and U.S. Special Operations Command (SOCOM) — anchors a military community that extends across Hillsborough County and into Pinellas and Pasco counties. Many of these veterans and their spouses are over 65 and living on fixed retirement incomes or VA disability benefits.

The critical gap that too few Tampa veterans plan for: the VA’s burial allowance ($300 for transportation, up to $991 for plot interment) has not kept pace with rising funeral costs. Meanwhile, Tampa funeral costs continue rising. A traditional funeral with burial now averages $9,000–$12,000 in the Tampa Bay area, with cremation services costing $6,000–$9,000.

For veterans eligible for burial at Florida National Cemetery in Bushnell (50 miles northeast of Tampa), the grave site and opening/closing fees are provided at no charge. But the funeral service, casket or urn, embalming, obituary, death certificates, and any private marker remain the family’s responsibility. A $10,000 final expense policy covers these costs entirely and leaves a small surplus.

Another Tampa-specific factor: the snowbird population. Hillsborough County’s 65+ population swells during winter months as seasonal residents from the Northeast and Midwest arrive. For these dual-residence seniors, a final expense policy from a national carrier with strong Florida licensing ensures smooth claims processing regardless of where death occurs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will my final expense policy conflict with VA burial benefits?

A: No. VA burial benefits and final expense insurance are completely independent. The VA pays the burial allowance to the person who incurred funeral costs; the final expense death benefit is paid directly to the named beneficiary. Your family keeps both.

Q: If I’m a snowbird with a primary residence in another state, should I buy a Florida policy?

A: It depends. If you spend more than half the year in Tampa, a Florida-issued policy provides local claims support and avoids potential residency-status disputes. Many planners recommend a small Florida policy in addition to any home-state coverage.

Q: Can I get final expense coverage if I have COPD, diabetes, or heart disease?

A: Yes, but the plan type varies. Well-managed conditions may qualify for level benefit at standard rates. Moderate conditions push you to graded benefit with a 2-year waiting period. For serious conditions, guaranteed issue is available with no health questions but a 2-year graded death benefit.

Q: Does final expense insurance build cash value?

A: Yes. Final expense policies are whole life insurance, meaning they build cash value over time. The cash value grows tax-deferred and can be accessed via policy loans or surrenders. For Tampa seniors, this is a secondary benefit — the primary purpose remains funeral cost coverage.
