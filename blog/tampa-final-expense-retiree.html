Unlike term life, final expense policies never expire as long as premiums are paid.

1. What Is Final Expense Insurance and Why Tampa Seniors Need It

Final expense insurance is a whole life policy with a smaller death benefit, designed to cover funeral and burial costs. Premiums are fixed and never increase, and the policy builds cash value over time.

Tampa-specific reasons to consider final expense:

  • 20% Hillsborough County veterans — many believe VA burial benefits are sufficient, but they often fall short
  • MacDill AFB military retirees — significant population of retired service members who may have partial VA benefits but still need gap coverage
  • Snowbird influx — thousands of seasonal residents over 65 who may not have updated their end-of-life planning
  • Rapid 65+ population growth — Tampa's senior population is growing faster than the national average
  • High Florida funeral costs — averaging $7,000-$10,000, which can devastate a fixed-income family
  • Medicare doesn't cover funerals — a common misconception that leads to unplanned financial burdens

For a Tampa senior on a fixed income, a small monthly premium ($30-$100/month) ensures that their family won't have to scramble to cover funeral expenses during an already difficult time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do VA burial benefits compare with private final expense insurance for Tampa veterans?

A: VA burial benefits provide a free plot in a national cemetery (such as Florida National Cemetery in Bushnell), a government headstone or marker, a burial flag, and a $300 burial allowance for non-service-connected deaths. However, these benefits do NOT cover the actual funeral service, viewing, transportation of remains, or cremation costs. A private final expense policy of $7,000-$15,000 fills the gap that VA benefits leave. For the 20% of Hillsborough County residents who are veterans, the smartest approach is often a combination: maximize VA benefits and supplement with a private final expense policy from VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840).

Q: What's the difference between simplified issue and guaranteed issue final expense?

A: Simplified issue requires you to answer 5-10 health questions (e.g., have you been diagnosed with cancer, had a heart attack, or been in a nursing home in the last 2 years?). If you qualify, coverage is immediate and premiums are lower. Guaranteed issue requires NO health questions and accepts everyone ages 50-85, but has a graded death benefit — typically 100% of premiums paid back plus 10% interest if death occurs in the first 2-3 years from natural causes (full benefit from day one for accidental death). For Tampa seniors with significant health issues, guaranteed issue is often the only option. VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) can help you decide which path works best.

Q: Will final expense insurance cover pre-existing conditions?

A: It depends on the product. Simplified issue policies ask about specific conditions (typically cancer, heart disease, stroke, COPD, diabetes complications, Alzheimer's). If you have one of these conditions and were diagnosed or treated in the lookback period (usually 2-3 years), you may be declined or offered a graded benefit instead. Guaranteed issue policies accept everyone regardless of pre-existing conditions — but with the graded death benefit limitation during the first 2-3 years. Mutual of Omaha and Aetna are two carriers available through VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) that have particularly favorable pre-existing condition guidelines for Florida residents.

Q: Can a Tampa couple get one joint final expense policy, or do they need separate policies?

A: Most carriers issue individual policies only. Each spouse should have their own policy with their own coverage amount. This is actually better because: (1) if one spouse passes, the other still has their own coverage; (2) underwriting is independent — one spouse's health issues won't affect the other's coverage; (3) each policy builds its own cash value. Some carriers like Foresters Financial offer spousal discounts or dual-policy savings when both spouses apply at the same time. VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) can help Tampa couples coordinate their coverage.

Q: Does Medicare or Medicare Supplement (Medigap) cover funeral costs?

A: No. Medicare covers medically necessary healthcare services, hospital stays, and doctor visits — but it does not cover funeral, burial, or cremation costs under any circumstance. Many Tampa seniors mistakenly believe Medicare will handle end-of-life expenses. A Medicare Supplement (Medigap) policy helps with coinsurance and deductibles for medical care but similarly offers no funeral coverage. Final expense insurance is specifically designed to fill this gap.
