Orlando-Specific Considerations

Orlando isn't just a tourism town anymore. The metro has become the top family relocation destination in Florida, with over 70,000 new residents arriving annually — and 55% of them are in the prime life-insurance-buying ages of 25–44. These are young professionals taking jobs at AdventHealth, Lockheed Martin, Siemens, Universal's new Epic Universe expansion, and the growing tech corridor along State Road 417.

The housing numbers tell the story. More than 50,000 new homes were permitted across Central Florida in 2025, concentrated in master-planned communities in Horizon West, Lake Nona, and the fast-growing suburbs of Osceola and Polk counties. These new homeowners are carrying 30-year mortgages at 6.5–7% interest, typically with 5–10% down. A family buying a $400,000 home with 5% down has a $380,000 mortgage — and property taxes and insurance in Orange County add another $6,000–$7,000 annually in carrying costs.

The insurance gap is real: most of these families have employer-provided group life at 1× salary (typically $65,000–$95,000), which covers roughly 2–4 months of household expenses. The mortgage, car payments, and childcare costs ($12,000–$18,000/year per child in metro Orlando) would drain that benefit in a quarter without additional coverage.

How to Get Coverage in Florida

1. Assess your family's coverage need — Multiply your annual income by 10–12× and add your mortgage balance. For a typical Orlando household earning $80,000 with a $380,000 mortgage, that's $1.18M in total need (10× income + mortgage).

2. Choose your term length — If you're under 40 and own a home with a 30-year mortgage, choose a 30-year term. The additional $15–$20/month over a 20-year term buys a decade of extra protection.

3. Complete simplified underwriting — In Florida, policies up to $1M can be issued with a short health questionnaire and no medical exam. Approval takes 24–72 hours.

4. Lock in your rate — Term life premiums are guaranteed level for the full term. Your rate won't increase even if your health changes.

5. Add valuable riders — Consider a child term rider ($10–$20/year per $10k of coverage) and waiver of premium ($2–$5/month) for comprehensive family protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is term life insurance in Florida affected by hurricane risk?

A: No. Life insurance premiums are based on age, health, and tobacco use — not geographic risk factors like hurricanes or flood zones. An Orlando resident and a Denver resident with identical health profiles pay the same rate.

Q: Can I get term life in Orlando if I work in the tourism industry with variable income?

A: Yes. Term life premiums are based on your age and health at application, not your income. As long as you can pay the monthly premium, your coverage stays in force. This makes term life ideal for hospitality workers, independent contractors, and seasonal employees in Orlando's tourism economy.

Q: What happens to my term policy if I move out of Florida?

A: Your policy is fully portable — it stays in force regardless of where you live. Your premium and death benefit are locked in at issue. Simply update your address with the carrier.

Q: Can I convert term life to permanent coverage in the future?

A: Most term life policies include a conversion feature that allows you to convert to a permanent policy (whole life or universal life) without new medical underwriting. This is valuable for Orlando families whose health or income may change over the policy term.
