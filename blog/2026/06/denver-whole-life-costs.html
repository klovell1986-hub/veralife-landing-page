1. What Is Whole Life Insurance and Why Denver Families Need It

Whole life insurance is a permanent life insurance policy that provides a guaranteed death benefit for your entire lifetime and accumulates cash value on a tax-deferred basis. Unlike term life — which covers you for a set period (10, 20, or 30 years) and pays nothing if you outlive it — whole life builds an asset inside the policy that you can borrow against or withdraw.

5. How to Buy Whole Life Insurance in Colorado

Step 1: Determine your need. Calculate the gap between your existing life insurance and what whole life would cover for estate liquidity, cash accumulation, or legacy goals.

Step 2: Choose a coverage amount. Select a face value that matches your purpose — $100k for final expense and legacy, $250k–$500k for cash accumulation and estate planning, $1M+ for estate tax funding.

Step 3: Select payment structure. Whole life offers several premium payment options:

  • Life pay: Level premiums for life (lowest annual outlay)
  • 10-pay: Premiums paid over 10 years (higher annual, but policy is paid up quickly)
  • 20-pay: Premiums paid over 20 years
  • Single premium: One lump sum (maximum cash value growth, but large upfront cost)

Step 4: Complete the application. For policies above $500k, a paramedical exam is typically required. For smaller policies ($100k–$500k), simplified underwriting may be available.

FAQ — Whole Life Insurance in Denver

What's the difference between whole life and term life insurance?

Term life covers you for a set period (10–30 years) with lower premiums but no cash value. Whole life covers you for your entire life, builds cash value tax-deferred, and has level premiums. For Denver professionals who want permanent coverage and tax-efficient accumulation, whole life is the right choice. VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) recommends evaluating both.

How does whole life cash value work?

Each premium payment is split — part goes to the cost of insurance and policy expenses, and the rest goes into a cash value account that grows tax-deferred at a guaranteed minimum rate. Dividend-paying mutual policies may add additional (non-guaranteed) growth. You can borrow against the cash value through policy loans at competitive interest rates, typically 5–8%.

Is a whole life policy portable if I move out of Colorado?

Yes. Whole life is a portable, individual policy — it stays with you regardless of where you live. If you move from Denver to another state, your premium, cash value, and death benefit remain unchanged. VeraLife Insurance Group (NPN 21426840) is licensed in 17 states, so your agent relationship can continue regardless of your move.

What's the best age to buy whole life insurance?

The earlier, the better. Premiums are locked in at your age at issue, so a 30-year-old Denver professional pays significantly less than a 45-year-old for the same coverage. At age 30, a $250k whole life policy costs $195–$275/month. At age 45, the same policy costs $370–$530/month — roughly 70% more.

Are policy loans taxable?

Policy loans are generally not taxable as long as the policy stays in force. However, if the policy lapses with an outstanding loan balance, the loan amount may be treated as taxable income to the extent it exceeds your cost basis. Always consult a tax advisor before taking policy loans.

What's the difference between mutual and stock insurance companies?

Mutual companies are owned by policyholders and pay dividends when the company performs well. Stock companies are owned by shareholders and may offer lower initial premiums but do not pay dividends to policyholders. For whole life policies held long-term, mutual carriers typically deliver higher total policy value.

Do I need a medical exam for whole life?

For coverage amounts over $500k, yes — a paramedical exam is typically required. For policies $100k–$500k, some carriers offer simplified underwriting with health questions only. The exam is free and can be done at a Denver-area clinic or in your home.
